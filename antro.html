What is Antro?

Antro is a 2.5D rhythm action platformer set in a stylised underground city pulsing with urban music. The player navigates layered environments, solves puzzles, and fights through a totalitarian regime, all synchronized to the beat of an original soundtrack.

The Context

Joining a 3-Year Project in Its Final Sprint

Most producers join a project from day one. I joined Antro mid-final-sprint on a title that had been in development for three years. The team had established workflows, pre-existing dynamics, and a publisher deadline already in motion.

The challenge wasn't starting something new. It was integrating fast enough to immediately add value without disrupting a team that had been working together for years, while coordinating an external QA company and a publisher simultaneously.

My Work

What I Owned

Team Leadership & Integration
Managing 10+ developers on a live, late-stage production

I stepped into a team that had been building their own way of working for three years. Rather than imposing a new system, I mapped their workflow first, spending the first days understanding their communication patterns, Notion structure, sprint cadence, and interpersonal dynamics.

  • Managed a cross-functional team of 10+ developers (programmers, artists, designers) through the final production phase and into certification
  • Acted as the primary communication bridge between the Lead Producer and the full development team, translating strategy into daily execution
  • Organised sprint planning sessions and milestone reviews to keep the team aligned on deliverables against the publisher timeline
  • Developed a production plan for the Gold version, breaking the final months into trackable milestones and assigning ownership to each deliverable
Why it mattered: On a project this far into development, morale, momentum, and clarity are the only real levers a producer has. Getting those right without slowing anyone down was the job.
External QA Coordination
Leading all communication with the external QA company through certification

I was the sole point of contact between Gatera Studio and the external QA company, managing the full bug pipeline from initial report through developer fix and final sign-off.

  • Triaged incoming bug reports, assigning priority and ownership to the right developer on the same day they arrived
  • Tracked resolution status in real-time, escalating blocking issues directly to the Lead Producer
  • Wrote and maintained the bug-tracking workflow in Notion so QA, developers, and the producer could always see the same picture
  • Ensured all critical gameplay issues were resolved before the Gold submission deadline
Publisher Relations & External Communications
Managing the studio's outbound email and publication offers

Beyond internal production, I handled the studio's external-facing communication, a responsibility that required discretion, clarity, and understanding of the publishing landscape.

  • Managed the company's email inbox, reviewing and drafting responses to inbound publication offers and partnership enquiries
  • Evaluated incoming offers against the project's current status and commercial goals, surfacing relevant opportunities to the Lead Producer with context and recommendations
  • Maintained a professional tone across all external communication, representing the studio to third parties
Production Documentation
Auditing and restructuring Notion & Miro for a team mid-sprint

Three years of development meant three years of accumulated documentation. I audited, restructured, and maintained the production infrastructure so the team could move fast in the final stretch without losing critical context.

  • Reorganised the Notion production hub â€” separating active tasks from archived work and creating a clear milestone tracker visible to the whole team
  • Updated Miro boards to reflect the current state of the project, removing outdated flows that were creating confusion
  • Established a documentation standard for the Gold phase so any team member could find the status of any task without asking
